# Open Questions & Next Steps

Research can narrow the field, but a handful of decisions are yours to make — and a few of them
change which rigs are even candidates. These are the questions worth talking through as a couple
(most of them over one dinner, honestly), roughly in order of how much they constrain everything
downstream.

## The big five

1. **What's the actual budget?** The docket found used 30–35' gas Class A rigs spanning roughly
   $55K (older, higher-mileage) to $110K+ (2022+, low miles), before ~$5–15K of gear and
   upgrades. A number — even a range — turns the model shortlist from "interesting" into "actionable."
   Remember to reserve budget for day-one gear, the F-53 handling package, inspection, taxes,
   and the first year of insurance/storage.

2. **Where do the kids sit while the coach is moving?** This is the highest-stakes finding in
   the docket ([doc 5](05-family-and-kids.html)): most Class A living-area seating cannot safely
   take a car seat. The realistic options are (a) shortlist only floorplans with forward-facing,
   chassis-anchored belted positions and have a CPST verify them, or (b) run a two-vehicle
   setup where the kids ride in the family car — which also solves bike hauling and
   campsite errands, at the cost of splitting the family on drive days. This decision should
   be made *before* falling in love with a floorplan.

3. **Tow a car (or trailer), or not?** Interacts directly with #2 and with the bikes
   ([doc 8](08-carrying-bikes.html)): the single rear hitch can carry a bike rack *or* a toad,
   not both. A small enclosed trailer solves bikes-plus-gear but adds length and complexity.

4. **What's the travel pattern, really?** Weekends-plus-a-few-big-trips vs. multi-week summer
   tours changes the campground-membership math ([doc 10](10-ownership-costs.html)) and how much
   boondocking investment ([doc 7](07-power-boondocking.html)) makes sense. Note: the kids
   presumably start kindergarten around fall 2027 — that puts a school calendar around travel
   right in the middle of the purchase window, and it's worth deciding whether that pushes the
   purchase earlier (travel freely in 2027) or later (no rush if travel gets calendar-bound anyway).

5. **Where does it live when it's home?** A 33–35' coach needs indoor/outdoor storage
   (NJ costs in [doc 10](10-ownership-costs.html)) unless the driveway and local rules allow it.
   Worth confirming before purchase, not after.

## Worth settling before serious shopping

- **Will both adults drive it?** If yes, both should do the driving course and the rental
  ([doc 13](13-rent-before-you-buy.html)). If no, that's a real constraint on trip design.
- **WFH ground rules:** how many hours of video calls per day, and how firm is the employer
  about location? That sets the redundancy budget in [doc 6](06-wfh-connectivity.html) —
  a paycheck-grade internet stack (Starlink + cellular backup + router) is roughly a
  $1,000–1,500 setup plus ~$150–200/month.
- **Rent-first commitment:** the docket's strongest process recommendation. A one-week Class A
  rental in 2026 or early 2027 tests the 35' assumption, the kids' reality, the WFH reality,
  and the drive itself — for a fraction of a purchase mistake.
- **E-bikes anywhere in the future?** They roughly double rack weight requirements and change
  the bike-carrying answer.

## Things the fact-checkers couldn't fully confirm

These claims survived in the docs but with hedged wording — treat them as "probably right,
verify before relying on it": dealer-vs-private price gaps (~$15–20K claimed), the exact
dual-monitor workstation power draw (150–250W range), professionally-installed lithium system
pricing ($14.5K+ quotes), the "90% of hitch racks aren't RV-rated" figure, NRVIA inspection
pricing ($400–900), and crosswind speed thresholds for a Class A. Current tire-age replacement
guidance (5 vs. 7 years) varies by manufacturer — check the tire maker's own bulletin for
whatever is on the rig you're inspecting.

## A suggested path from here to purchase

1. **Now–fall 2026:** read the docket together; settle the big five above; start watching
   RV Trader listings for the shortlist models to calibrate real asking prices (no buying,
   just watching).
2. **Fall 2026–spring 2027:** rent a 33–35' gas Class A for a week ([doc 13](13-rent-before-you-buy.html));
   both adults take an RV driving course; visit an RV show to walk many floorplans in one day.
3. **When the rental confirms the plan:** lock the floorplan requirements (kid seating first),
   shortlist 2–3 models, and start shopping seriously — off-season (late fall/winter) is the
   buyer's market.
4. **On any serious candidate:** NRVIA inspection, weight-sticker check against the payload
   math in [doc 3](03-chassis-weights-payload.html), tire DOT dates, and the negotiation
   playbook in [doc 4](04-used-buying-inspection.html).

# The Master Reading List

This is the aggregate bibliography for the whole docket. Every doc (01–14, 17–24) now
carries its own inline citations and a per-doc **Sources** section — this page collects
every external link cited anywhere in the docket, deduped by URL, so you don't have to
hunt doc-by-doc to find something again.

Primary/official sources (statutes, federal agencies, manufacturer spec pages) are the
most trustworthy layer. Dealer and industry blogs are next. Forum/community threads
(iRV2, Facebook-style anecdotes) are labeled where they appear — real owner experience,
not verified data. Where a doc's Sources section explicitly flags a claim as unverified
or disputed, that flag is preserved in the one-line description here.

Total unique external links in this reading list: **503**.

## Start here (~15 most decision-relevant sources)

If you only read fifteen links before a dealer visit or an offer, read these — they
carry the numbers and rules that actually change what you buy or how you drive it.

- [FMVSS motorhome cargo-carrying-capacity labeling rule, 2007 (govinfo — primary federal text)](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/FR-2007-12-04/html/E7-22962.htm) — the federal rule behind every OCCC/payload number on the yellow sticker; read this before trusting any published cargo-capacity figure.
- [NIRVC: RV Numbers — Ratings, Limits & Capacity](https://www.nirvc.com/blog/rv-numbers-ratings-limits-and-capacity/) — the one-page plain-English decoder for UVW/GVWR/GAWR/GCWR/CCC/OCCC, the vocabulary the whole docket assumes.
- [iRV2: OCCC of Winnebago 2021 models under 30ft (community)](https://www.irv2.com/threads/occc-of-winnebago-2021-models-under-30ft.1956850/) — real owner-reported OCCC numbers showing a ~2:1 spread within the same model line — the reason to verify payload per-VIN, not per-brochure.
- [CPST Fact Sheet: RVs and Child Occupants (PDF), Pro Car Seat Safety](https://procarseatsafety.com/uploads/3/5/3/6/35362606/fact_sheet_rv___child_occupants__cpsts_2023.pdf) — the single best source on why RV living-area seating isn't crash-tested and where the kids should actually sit while moving.
- [N.J.S.A. 39:3-76.2a via FindLaw](https://codes.findlaw.com/nj/title-39-motor-vehicles-and-traffic-regulation/nj-st-sect-39-3-76-2a/) — the actual New Jersey child-restraint statute that governs how the twins ride, legally.
- [Lichtsinn RV — Class A Motorhomes: Diesel Pusher vs Gas Motorhome](https://www.lichtsinn.com/blog/diesel-pusher-versus-gas-motorhome/) — the clearest single side-by-side of gas-vs-diesel price, mpg, maintenance, and resale that the whole gas-vs-diesel debate rests on.
- [SuperSteer — Ford F-53 chassis overview](https://supersteerparts.com/ford-f53-chassis) — the chassis under nearly every gas coach on the shortlist; GVWR tiers, handling quirks, and the V10-to-V8 engine transition in one page.
- [RV Trader — Winnebago Class A listings](https://www.rvtrader.com/Winnebago-Class-A/rvs-for-sale?type=Class+A%7C198067&make=Winnebago%7C2307464) — live used-market pricing reality check against every "typical price" figure quoted in the docket.
- [Michelin RV/Motorhome Tire Service Life Guide (PDF)](https://www.michelinb2b.com/wps/b2bcontent/PDF/3_TB_Service_Life_RV_Motorhome.pdf) — the manufacturer's own age-based tire-replacement guidance (5-year inspection, 10-year replacement) — the single most expensive "surprise" line item on a used buy.
- [NRVIA — National RV Inspectors Association](https://nrvia.org/) — the certifying body for the pre-purchase inspection you should never skip; where to find a real inspector.
- [NJ MVC — Mobile Homes, Motor Homes and Station Wagons](https://www.nj.gov/mvc/vehicles/motorhomes.htm) — the primary NJ titling/registration/insurance rules that apply the moment you buy, in-state or out.
- [MIRC — Starlink for Mobile RV and Boat Use](https://www.rvmobileinternet.com/guides/starlink-rv-boat/) — the specialist reference for the connectivity setup the WFH requirement actually depends on.
- [Battle Born Batteries — Boondocking 101: sizing an off-grid system](https://battlebornbatteries.com/blogs/articles/rv-boondocking-101-sizing-an-off-grid-power-system) — the method for sizing the battery/solar system that determines whether "real boondocking" is actually achievable.
- [Let's Go Aero: What Makes a Bike Rack RV Approved?](https://www.letsgoaero.com/blogs/resources/what-makes-bike-rack-rv-approved) — why car-rated bike racks fail on an RV and what to look for instead, directly relevant to the 6-bike requirement.
- [RVezy: RV Insurance Cost Guide 2026](https://www.rvezy.com/blog/rv-insurance-costs) — the clearest premium-band overview across recreational vs. full-time use, which frames the ongoing-cost math.

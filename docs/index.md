# The Muth Family RV Docket

Research for buying a **used motorhome** for two adults and two kids (4 now — 5 and 6 by the
time we'd actually buy), with full-time remote work aboard, mostly-campground travel plus real
boondocking capability, and room for six bikes. The working budget is **$100–150k financed**,
flexible if the right deal shows up. Purchase window: **late 2026 through 2028**, so there's
time to do this right.

The primary target is still a **gas Class A, 30–35 feet** — but this revision widens the lens
on purpose: **large Class C rigs (30'+)** are now on the table, **diesel is no longer ruled
out** (informed-buyer coverage, not a change of heart), and **towing a car** gets a full
work-up instead of a footnote. Home base: Brick, NJ.

## How much to trust this

Every doc now carries **inline source citations plus a Sources section** — click through and
vet anything before relying on it. The docket was written by research agents in July 2026,
then fact-checked twice: an adversarial pass on each doc's highest-stakes claims, then a
second round of legal, money, vehicle-spec, and consistency audits. Anything that couldn't be
confirmed is hedged in the text and collected in
[Decisions & Open Questions](16-open-questions.html). Where rules are involved, docs separate
**what the law requires** from **what experts recommend** — they are not the same thing.
It's still research, not gospel: **verify current prices, specs, and laws before signing anything.**

## Where to start

If you two read only four docs together, read these — they carry the decisions:

1. **[RVing With Kids (Ages 4–6)](05-family-and-kids.html)** — where the kids legally and
   safely sit while driving is the single biggest constraint on which rigs are candidates.
   This version separates NJ law (cited statute) from safety guidance, and corrects the
   earlier front-seat suggestion.
2. **[Towing a Car](19-towing-a-car.html)** — the headline finding of this revision: the
   RAV4 and the Tucson are almost certainly **trailer-only** (neither flat-tows), which
   reshapes the toad question entirely.
3. **[The One-Driver Question](22-one-driver-family.html)** — if Sara doesn't want to drive
   a Class A, this doc covers training, whether a Class C is genuinely easier, and the
   two-vehicle caravan option that can dissolve the car-seat *and* toad problems at once.
4. **[Rent Before You Buy](13-rent-before-you-buy.html)** — the agreed plan, now a concrete
   protocol: where to rent near Brick, what it costs, and exactly what to test that week.

## The one-paragraph takeaway

The plan is sound and now broader: a used coach in the $100–150k band buys real choice — a
newer gas Class A, a large Class C, or (with eyes open) an older diesel pusher. The four
things that deserve the most respect: **where the kids sit while driving** (law first, then
crashworthiness — it constrains floorplans more than anything else), **the towing question**
(our current cars almost certainly can't be flat-towed, which makes the two-vehicle caravan
a serious contender), **cargo weight capacity** (it varies wildly between rigs that look
identical), and **a professional inspection before any purchase** (water damage is the
killer, and it hides). The rental week is the cheap test of everything.

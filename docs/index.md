# The Muth Family RV Docket

Research for buying a **used, gasoline Class A motorhome, 30–35 feet**, for two adults and two
4-year-olds — with full-time remote work aboard, mostly-campground travel plus real boondocking
capability, and room for six bikes. Purchase window: **late 2026 through 2028**, so there's time
to do this right.

This docket was compiled in July 2026 by a fan-out of research agents, then fact-checked: 79
high-stakes claims (safety, legal, money) went through an adversarial verification pass — 9 were
refuted and corrected, and anything that couldn't be confirmed is hedged in the text. It's still
research, not gospel. **Verify current prices, specs, and laws before signing anything.**

cargo capacity faster than you'd think.

## The one-paragraph takeaway

The plan is sound: a used gas Class A in the 30–35' range is the right value play for this use
case, the market in 2026–2028 favors patient buyers, and every capability you want (WFH, six
bikes, boondocking) is achievable with upgrades a handy owner can do. The three things that
deserve the most respect: **where the kids sit while driving** (it constrains floorplan choice
more than anything else), **cargo weight capacity** (it varies wildly between rigs that look
identical), and **a professional inspection before any purchase** (water damage is the killer,
and it hides).

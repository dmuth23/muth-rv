# The Muth Family RV Docket

Research for buying a **used gasoline Class C motorhome** — two adults, two homeschooled kids
(4 now, 5–6 by purchase time), full-time remote work aboard, six bikes, and one mission above
the rest: **national and state park camping**, from the Jersey shore out to Sequoia and
Canyonlands. Budget **$60–90k financed**; window **late 2026–2028** — and, deliberately,
*maybe not at all*: the docket takes the case against buying as seriously as the case for it.

## How much to trust this

Every doc carries inline source citations plus a Sources section — click through and vet
anything. Compiled and adversarially fact-checked July 2026; what couldn't be confirmed is
hedged in the text and gathered in [Decisions & Open Questions](16-open-questions.html), and
docs always separate **what the law requires** from **what experts recommend**. It's research,
not gospel: **verify current limits, prices, and laws before booking or buying** — park rules
especially change season to season.

## The one-paragraph takeaway

The parks mission makes the shape of the answer clear: a used gas Class C, short enough that
the gate at Squaw Flat and the switchbacks on the Generals Highway are invitations instead of
problems, big enough that four people and two careers can live in it for a week at a time —
the data says that window is real but narrow (roughly 27–30 ft), and the rental week exists to
find our spot in it. The number that decides everything isn't a price, it's **nights per
year**: below roughly 44, renting wins and that's a perfectly good outcome. And whichever way
it goes, the travel starts now, not on delivery day — the trips we take while deciding are the
point, not the preview. That's a fortunate set of problems to have, and the docket tries to
treat them that way.
